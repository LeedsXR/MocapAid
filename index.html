A-pose Guide

A-pose Troubleshooting

The A-pose is critical for proper motion capture initialization. Ensure your subject stands with:

  • Arms extended about 45Â° from the body
  • Palms facing down
  • Feet shoulder-width apart
  • Toes pointing forward
  • Back straight
  • Head looking forward

Common issues include:

  • Arms too high or too low
  • Hands not properly aligned
  • Feet positioned incorrectly
  • Poor posture affecting overall alignment

Range of Motion Guide

Shoulder ROM

Demonstrate full shoulder mobility by rotating arms in forward and backward circular motions. This helps ensure accurate tracking of shoulder and arm movements.

Neck ROM

Tilt the head forward, backward, and side to side to establish the full range of motion for the neck joints.

Hip ROM

Perform hip rotations and leg lifts to calibrate the lower body tracking and ensure accurate hip joint movement.
